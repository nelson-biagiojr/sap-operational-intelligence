# Common SAP Cutover Failures (and How to Avoid Them)

## Objective

Highlight the most frequent failure points observed during SAP cutover and go-live, and define practical mitigation strategies.

---

## 1. Misalignment Between Planning and Execution

### Problem
Cutover plan exists but is not followed in real execution.

### Impact
- Delays
- Confusion across teams
- Increased risk exposure

### Root Cause
- Lack of ownership
- Weak governance

### Mitigation
- Enforce cutover command structure
- Assign clear activity owners
- Real-time tracking in war room

---

## 2. Underestimated Integration Complexity

### Problem
Interfaces behave differently in production vs testing.

### Impact
- Data inconsistencies
- Process failures (OTC / PTP / RTR)

### Root Cause
- Incomplete end-to-end testing
- Missing real-world scenarios

### Mitigation
- Perform full integration validation
- Include business-driven scenarios
- Monitor interfaces in real time

---

## 3. Poor Stakeholder Communication

### Problem
Business teams are not aligned with cutover timing and constraints.

### Impact
- Operational disruptions
- Escalations
- Loss of trust

### Root Cause
- Over-reliance on technical communication
- Lack of structured updates

### Mitigation
- Establish communication cadence
- Use executive-level summaries
- Align expectations before go-live

---

## 4. Weak Incident Management During Hypercare

### Problem
Issues are not prioritized or escalated correctly.

### Impact
- SLA breaches
- Business impact escalation

### Root Cause
- No clear incident ownership
- Lack of classification model

### Mitigation
- Define incident severity levels
- Assign L1 / L2 / L3 ownership
- Track SLA adherence

---

## 5. Overconfidence Before Go-Live

### Problem
Team assumes everything is ready without validating critical scenarios.

### Impact
- Major issues during go-live
- Emergency fixes

### Root Cause
- Pressure to meet deadlines
- Lack of structured validation

### Mitigation
- Enforce Go/No-Go criteria
- Execute structured smoke tests
- Validate critical business flows

---

## 6. Lack of Clear Decision-Making Structure

### Problem
Decisions are delayed during critical moments.

### Impact
- Increased downtime
- Escalation chaos

### Root Cause
- Undefined authority
- Multiple decision points

### Mitigation
- Define escalation matrix
- Empower cutover leadership
- Centralize decisions in war room

---

## Key Takeaway

Successful cutover is not only about planning —  
it is about execution discipline, communication, and rapid decision-making.

---

## Positioning

This analysis is based on real-world SAP program experience in high-criticality environments.
